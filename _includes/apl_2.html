Advanced Computer Vision for the Visually Impaired

I led the computer vision algorithm design for a retinal prosthesis system, enabling visually impaired patients to recognize and locate objects using auditory and visual feedback with the prosthesis. This innovative system was successfully tested on patients, and result in two patents (US patents 10,265,218 and 10,013,599). The technology was a significant step forward in visual prosthesis research, demonstrating the potential for computer vision and machine learningto improve the lives of the visually impaired.
